About this project

Kenyon College's Gund Gallery is a world-class art museum in Gambier, Ohio. The gallery was established in 2011 and made possible through the Gund family's generous donation. The gallery is named in honor of George Gund, a trustee of Kenyon College from 1961 until his death in 1966. The Gund Gallery is a stunning example of contemporary architecture and is one of the most significant buildings on the Kenyon College campus. The gallery features over 10,000 square feet of exhibition space and has a collection of over 7,000 works of art. The collection includes pieces from various artists and art movements, including modern and contemporary art, as well as ancient and traditional art. One of the most impressive aspects of the Gund Gallery is its commitment to showcasing emerging and underrepresented artists. The gallery hosts several exhibitions each year focusing on the work of artists who have yet to be well-known or from historically marginalized communities. This commitment to diversity and inclusivity is reflected in the exhibitions and the gallery's educational programs and outreach efforts. The gallery's educational programs include tours, lectures, workshops, and other events designed to engage students and the broader community with the art on display.
